# Review of Probability Basics
**EEE 554 — Lecture #1**

## Terminology and Notation

* **"Universal outcome set" $S$**: The set of all possible outcomes of a specified random trial ("experiment").
* **"Outcome"**: An element of the set $S$.
* **$x \in A$**: "$x$ is an element of the set $A$."
* **$A \subset B$**: "$A$ is a subset of $B$."
* **$A \cup B = \{x \mid x \in A \text{ or } x \in B\}$**: "The union of $A$ and $B$."
* **$A \cap B = \{x \mid x \in A \text{ and } x \in B\}$**: "The intersection of $A$ and $B$."
* **$A - B = \{x \mid x \in A \text{ but } x \notin B\}$**: "The set difference $A$ minus $B$."
* **$\emptyset$**: "The empty set."
* **$\overline{A} = S - A$**: "The complement of $A$."

**"Events"**: A collection $\mathcal{F}$ of subsets of $S$ that satisfies the sigma field ($\sigma$-field) conditions:
1. $S \in \mathcal{F}$
2. If $A \in \mathcal{F}$ then $\overline{A} \in \mathcal{F}$
3. If $A_1, A_2, \dots \in \mathcal{F}$, then $A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \dots \in \mathcal{F}$

**Definition:** Events $A_1, A_2, \dots$ are **exhaustive** if $A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \dots = S$. They are **mutually exclusive** if $A_i \cap A_j = \emptyset$ for $i \neq j$.

---

## Probability Measure

A probability measure assigns a real number to each event; i.e., $P : \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$. It must satisfy:
1. $P(A) \ge 0$ for all events $A$
2. $P(S) = 1$
3. If $A_1, A_2, \dots$ are mutually exclusive events,
   $$P(A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \dots) = P(A_1) + P(A_2) + \dots$$

---

## Generation of $\sigma$-Fields

Beginning with a collection $\mathcal{B}$ of subsets of $S$, with $S \in \mathcal{B}$, form every set possible by applying $\sigma$-field properties (2) and (3) to the original collection $\mathcal{B}$. The new collection $\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{B}}$ will be a $\sigma$-field and is said to be "generated by $\mathcal{B}$."
If $S$ is a topological space (i.e., with open sets), the **Borel $\sigma$-field** is the $\sigma$-field generated by the open sets of $S$.

---

## Conditional Probability

The conditional probability of event $A$ given event $B$ is:
$$
P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}
$$

---

## Bayes' Rule

From the definition, $P(A \cap B) = P(A|B)P(B)$.
But also $P(A \cap B) = P(B|A)P(A)$. So,
$$
P(B|A)P(A) = P(A|B)P(B)
$$
or
$$
P(B|A) = \frac{P(A|B)P(B)}{P(A)}
$$

---

## Statistical Independence

Events $A$ and $B$ are independent if:
$$
P(A|B) = P(A)
$$
Independent events satisfy $\frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} = P(A)$, so:
$$
P(A \cap B) = P(A)P(B)
$$

---

## Total Probability Principle

Let $A_1, \dots, A_n$ be mutually exclusive and exhaustive events in $S$. If $B$ is any event, then $(B \cap A_1), \dots, (B \cap A_n)$ are mutually exclusive events with:
$$
B = (B \cap A_1) \cup \dots \cup (B \cap A_n)
$$
Thus,
$$
P(B) = P(B \cap A_1) + \dots + P(B \cap A_n)
$$
or
$$
P(B) = P(B|A_1)P(A_1) + \dots + P(B|A_n)P(A_n)
$$

---

## Probability of Union

Note that $A = (A - B) \cup (A \cap B)$ with $A - B$ and $A \cap B$ mutually exclusive. So:
$$
P(A - B) = P(A) - P(A \cap B)
$$
Similarly,
$$
P(B - A) = P(B) - P(A \cap B)
$$
Finally,
$$
A \cup B = (A - B) \cup (B - A) \cup (A \cap B)
$$
where the three events on the right-hand side are mutually exclusive. Thus,
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(A \cup B) &= P(A - B) + P(B - A) + P(A \cap B) \\
&= [P(A) - P(A \cap B)] + [P(B) - P(A \cap B)] + P(A \cap B) \\
&= P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)
\end{aligned}
$$
